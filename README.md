<section id="research">
<center>
<h2>Research Data</h2>
<img src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1576086213369-97a306d36557" alt="Research 1">
<img src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1530026405186-ed1f139313f8" alt="Research 2">
</center>

<p>
In our biology laboratory practices, we investigated the structure and function of cells, respiration, and microscopy. The microscope experiment allowed us to observe onion epidermis cells and protozoan cultures under different magnifications. We discovered that increasing magnification improves image detail, but decreases the field of view, making observation more difficult. We also learned how methylene blue stain helps improve visibility of cell structures.
</p>

<p>
Another important experiment focused on respiration and the effects of exercise. We used bromothymol blue and ammonia to study the amount of carbon dioxide exhaled before and after exercise. During the experiment, the solution changed color depending on the level of carbon dioxide present. This demonstrated the relationship between respiration, pH changes, and physical activity.
</p>

<p>
The respiratory system is responsible for inhaling oxygen and exhaling carbon dioxide. Exercise increases oxygen demand, causing faster breathing and greater carbon dioxide production. These observations helped us understand how biological systems react to physical activity and environmental changes.
</p>

<section id="problems">
<center>
<h2>Problems</h2>
<img src="https://img.rtve.es/n/1843720?w=1600" alt="Problem 1">
<img src="https://encrypted-tbn0.gstatic.com/images?q=tbn:ANd9GcRByYcBMRv9EmUSvVfBbgDc0OgLEj64xwyQHw&s" alt="Problem 2">
</center>

<p>
One important problem in healthcare is the difficulty of observing microscopic organisms and cells clearly. Scientists and doctors often require advanced microscopes to analyze bacteria, tissues, and microorganisms. High magnifications improve detail but reduce the field of vision, making the process more complicated.
</p>

<p>
Another healthcare problem involves respiratory diseases and breathing difficulties. Exercise, stress, pollution, and unhealthy habits can affect respiration and oxygen intake. During our respiration experiment, we observed how carbon dioxide production changed depending on physical activity. This can be related to conditions such as asthma or respiratory infections.
</p>

<p>
Possible solutions include the use of advanced robotic systems, better laboratory technology, and AI-assisted healthcare devices. Robots can help doctors perform more accurate diagnoses and monitor patients in real time. Smart systems can also improve microscopic observations and help detect diseases earlier.
</p>

<ul>
<li>Microscopic observation difficulties</li>
<li>Respiratory system disorders</li>
<li>Limited medical precision</li>
<li>Need for faster diagnosis</li>
</ul>
</section>

<section id="robotic">
<center>
<h2>Robotic Solution</h2>
<img src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1485827404703-89b55fcc595e" alt="Robot 1">
<img src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1507146153580-69a1fe6d8aa1" alt="Robot 2">
</center>

<p>
Our robotic solution is a healthcare nanorobot designed to help doctors detect diseases and monitor body functions. The robot could travel through the bloodstream and identify abnormal cells, infections, or dangerous microorganisms. It would use sensors and artificial intelligence to collect information and send data to medical professionals.
</p>

<p>
The nanorobot design includes microscopic cameras, sensors, and a communication system. The robot would also help improve precision in diagnosis and reduce risks during medical procedures. By using robotic technology, healthcare professionals can obtain faster and more accurate information about patient health.
</p>

<p>
The robot could also assist with respiratory monitoring by detecting changes in oxygen and carbon dioxide levels. This would be especially useful for patients with breathing problems or athletes who need constant monitoring during exercise.
</p>

<ol>
<li>Detect abnormal cells</li>
<li>Monitor respiration</li>
<li>Send health information</li>
<li>Support medical diagnosis</li>
</ol>
